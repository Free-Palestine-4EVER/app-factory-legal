Privacy Policy

Multitimer: Kitchen Timers

Last updated: July 17, 2026

Multitimer is built to work entirely on your device. This policy explains, plainly, what that means.

Data we collect

None. Multitimer does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data, usage analytics, advertising identifiers, or device identifiers. There is no account, no sign-in, and no tracking of any kind.

Network requests

The only network requests Multitimer makes are anonymous, unauthenticated calls to the free public recipe API TheMealDB to fetch recipe content (names, photos, ingredients, and instructions) that you browse or search for. These requests contain no personal information — just the recipe search term or id you requested. Recipe data and photos are cached directly on your device so the app keeps working fully offline afterward.

Data stored on your device

Your timers, favorites, shopping list, converter history, and settings are stored locally on your device only, using Apple's standard on-device storage. This data is never uploaded anywhere, is not visible to us, and stays on your device until you delete the app or clear it yourself in Settings.

Notifications

If you grant permission, Multitimer schedules local notifications on your device to alert you when a kitchen timer finishes — one per timer, so several running at once each alert independently. These notifications are generated and delivered entirely on-device via Apple's notification system — nothing is sent to any server.

Children's privacy

Multitimer does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children, because it collects no data from anyone at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the update will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
