Frequently Asked Questions

Does Moonwatch work without internet?
Yes — after the first launch, the moon phase, moonrise/moonset, the star map, and ISS tracking all work fully offline. The app only reaches out to the internet to silently refresh ISS orbital data in the background when you're connected.

Why does the app ask for my location?
Your location (or a manually entered one, in Settings) is required to compute your exact moonrise/moonset times and which stars, planets, and satellites are above your horizon right now.

How is the Moon phase rendered so precisely?
Moonwatch computes the Moon's real illuminated fraction and phase angle offline via the Meeus astronomical algorithms (the same math used in professional ephemeris software), then renders an exact geometric terminator — not a swap between 8 stock icons.

Why does the ISS position look slightly off?
ISS position accuracy depends on how recently its orbital data (TLE) was refreshed — accuracy degrades gradually over 1–2 weeks without a refresh. Open the ISS tab while online to refresh it.
