Moonwatch: Lunar Phases

Privacy Policy — last updated July 17, 2026
We collect nothing. There is no account, no server, no analytics.

Summary

Moonwatch is an offline moon-phase, star map, and ISS tracker app. It does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to us or to any third party. There is no user account, no sign-up, no advertising SDK, and no analytics SDK of any kind embedded in the app.

Location

Moonwatch uses your device's location (via Apple's CoreLocation, only with your permission) to compute the Moon's phase, rise/set times, the star map, and ISS visibility for where you are. This location data is processed entirely on your device and is never sent to us or to any third-party server. You may deny location access and enter a location manually instead — every feature still works.

Notifications

If you enable ISS pass alerts, Moonwatch schedules a local notification directly on your device using Apple's UserNotifications framework. No notification content or schedule is ever sent to a server — the alert is computed and delivered entirely on-device.

Network Requests

After first launch, Moonwatch works fully offline. The only network requests it ever makes are optional, silent refreshes of the public ISS orbital data (a "TLE") from wheretheiss.at and, as a fallback, celestrak.org — both public, keyless aerospace-data services. These requests contain no information about you: no identifiers, no location, no account data. If you are offline, the app simply keeps using its last cached orbital data.

Data Not Collected

In App Store terms: Moonwatch's data collection practice is Data Not Collected. We do not collect any data that is linked to your identity, and we do not track you across apps or websites owned by other companies.

Children

Moonwatch contains no user-generated content, chat, or social features, and is safe for all ages.
