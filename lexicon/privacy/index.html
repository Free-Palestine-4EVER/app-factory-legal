PRIVACY POLICY

Lexicon: Word of the Day

Last updated: July 17, 2026

Lexicon is a daily word, flashcard, and word-game vocabulary app. This policy explains, plainly, what data it handles.

Data we collect

None. Lexicon does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data, usage analytics, advertising identifiers, or device identifiers. There is no account, no sign-in, and no tracking of any kind.

Network requests

Lexicon ships with a bundled word list, cached definitions, and a game word bank, so the Word of the Day, Flashcards, and Word Game features all work fully offline from first launch. When your device is online, the app makes anonymous, keyless requests to two free public services to enrich word entries: the Free Dictionary API (definitions, phonetics, and audio pronunciation) and Datamuse (related words, synonyms, and antonyms). These requests contain only the word being looked up — never anything about you or your device — and every feature falls back silently to the bundled offline data if there is no connection.

Data stored on your device

Your flashcard progress, streak history, favorites, game scores, and settings are stored locally on your device only, using Apple's standard on-device storage. This data is never uploaded anywhere, is not visible to us, and stays on your device until you delete the app or clear it yourself in Settings.

Notifications

If you enable optional reminders (daily word, streak-at-risk, cards due), the scheduling happens entirely on your device via Apple's notification system. No notification data is sent to us or anyone else.

Children's privacy

Lexicon does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children, because it collects no data from anyone at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the update will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
