Frequently Asked Questions

Does Skywatch work without internet?
Yes — after the first launch, ISS tracking and pass prediction, the star map, and the moon phase all work fully offline, computed from a bundled orbital snapshot. The app only reaches out to the internet to silently refresh that orbital data (the "TLE") in the background when you're connected.

Why does the app ask for my location?
Your location (or a manually entered one, in Settings) is required to compute exactly when and where the ISS will be visible from where you are, and which stars and planets are above your horizon right now.

How does Skywatch know when the ISS will be visible?
Skywatch propagates the station's real orbital elements (SGP4/SDP4, the same math used by professional trackers) offline, then checks three conditions for every moment ahead: the station must be above your horizon, sunlit, and your sky must be dark enough (the Sun below -6°) — the standard definition of a "visible pass."

Why does the ISS position look slightly off?
Position accuracy depends on how recently the orbital data (TLE) was refreshed — accuracy degrades gradually over 1–2 weeks without a refresh. Open the app while online to refresh it.
