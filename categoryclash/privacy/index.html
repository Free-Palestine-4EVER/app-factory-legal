Privacy Policy

Category Clash: Trivia

Last updated: July 17, 2026

Category Clash: Trivia is built to work entirely on your device, with one narrow, optional exception explained below. This policy explains, plainly, what that means.

Data we collect

None. Category Clash: Trivia does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data, usage analytics, advertising identifiers, or device identifiers. There is no account, no sign-in, and no tracking of any kind.

Network requests

The app ships with a large bundled question bank and is fully playable offline, in every mode, from first launch. The only network requests the app can ever make are outbound calls to the free, open Open Trivia Database (opentdb.com) — and only in two cases: (1) an optional "refresh more questions" action you trigger yourself in Settings, and (2) the deep-linked "Category Clash: Trivia" quiz you launch, which uses Open Trivia Database to fetch questions for the topic you picked. These requests contain no personal information whatsoever — they are anonymous requests for trivia questions by category, difficulty, and quantity only. No cookies, no identifiers, no account, no analytics SDK, and no advertising SDK are present anywhere in the app.

Data stored on your device

Your scores, streaks, accuracy stats, per-category mastery, favorites, recently played categories, and settings are stored locally on your device only. This data is never uploaded anywhere, is not visible to us, and stays on your device until you delete the app or clear it yourself from Settings.

Notifications

If you turn on the optional daily-challenge reminder, Category Clash: Trivia schedules a local notification on your device using Apple's notification system. Nothing about this is sent to any server.

Children's privacy

Category Clash: Trivia does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children, because it collects no personal data from anyone at all.

Third-party content

Trivia questions are sourced from the free, open Open Trivia Database, used under its open license. Attribution is shown in Settings → Credits.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the update will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
