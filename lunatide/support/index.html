Frequently Asked Questions

Does Luna Tide work without internet?
Yes — the Sky tab's moon phase, illumination, full/new moon countdowns, day length, and golden/blue-hour windows are computed entirely offline. Weather, hourly forecast, and air quality show your last cached data (with an "as of" time) when offline, and refresh automatically once you're back online.

Why does the app ask for my location?
Your location (or a manually searched city, if you prefer) is used to show accurate weather, air quality, moon phase, day length, and golden-hour times for that spot. You can deny location access and search any city worldwide instead.

How accurate is the moon-phase countdown?
Luna Tide computes the Moon's phase and illumination offline using the standard days-since-new-moon method, accurate to within a few hours — great for everyday use, though not full ephemeris-grade precision.

What does the "Sea level & waves" card mean?
For coastal cities, Luna Tide shows real sea-level-height and wave data from Open-Meteo's marine API. This is live ocean-state data, not a harmonic tide-table prediction, and it's hidden automatically for inland cities.

Where does the weather and air-quality data come from?
Luna Tide uses the free, open Open-Meteo APIs for forecast, air quality, geocoding, and marine data. See the in-app Credits screen for full attribution.
